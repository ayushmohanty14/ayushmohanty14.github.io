I am a Ph.D. candidate in Machine Learning at the H. Milton Stewart School of Industrial and Systems Engineering at the Georgia Institute of Technology, advised by Prof. Nagi Gebraeel. My research focuses on decentralized learning and causal reasoning in interconnected cyber-physical systems. Specifically, I work on theoretical foundations for causal learning and inference in distributed and heterogeneous settings, with an emphasis on causal discovery, uncertainty quantification, counterfactual reasoning, and root cause analysis under anomaly.

A brief background: I graduated from the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Kharagpur with a Bachelor’s degree in Manufacturing Science and Engineering and a Master’s in Industrial Engineering and Management. Building on this foundation in manufacturing, I transitioned into machine learning for large-scale industrial systems. This interdisciplinary experience now informs my research on data-driven methods for complex engineering environments, including cyber-physical systems.

Research Overview

My research program is organized around two complementary directions: Federated Learning and Causal Reasoning, and Predictive Modeling in Dynamical Systems. Across both, I study how learning, inference, and decision-making should be carried out when data is distributed, systems are interdependent, and operational constraints make centralization infeasible.

Past Experience

Prior to and during my Ph.D., I have worked across academia, national laboratories, and industry on problems in machine learning, optimization, and cyber-physical systems. Working as a PhD intern in Argonne National Laboratory, I developed predictive models for degradation and remaining useful life estimation in energy systems using real-time data. I also held a visiting research position at the Warwick Manufacturing Group, where I worked on data-driven analysis of industrial X-ray CT data, and at the University of Alberta, where I designed intelligent, Industry 4.0-aligned system prototypes for automated aquaponic industry. During my undergraduate internship at MathWorks India, I developed algorithmic solutions for UAV path planning.
